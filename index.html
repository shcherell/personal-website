The Difference Between a Team and Specialists Working on the Same Project

A true team is characterized by high motivation and a shared interest in the result. Team members collaborate to overcome obstacles, and each contributes to the project's success, making teamwork more efficient and rewarding. However, challenges arise when subgroups within a team exclude others from decision-making, or the team's goals diverge from the project's objectives. These issues can stem from dissatisfaction with management, internal conflicts, disagreement with project goals, or problems with deadlines and budgets. Understanding the root cause of team issues is not just important; it's crucial. It's the first step towards effective problem-solving. Addressing these concerns might involve improving communication, realigning the team with the project goals, or, in some cases, making difficult decisions about team composition.

What can help unite specialists into a team? The organization's culture and the team's culture (each with its own internal rituals and traditions) contribute to the development of a sense of belonging and shared values among team members. It is a good decision to make a list of measures for the year that can be gradually implemented to start managing the culture. In many teams, there is a key individual who acts as the glue that holds the team together. If this person leaves, it's crucial to arrange a timely replacement. Failure to do so can lead to team disintegration and a decline in productivity, often without a clear understanding of the cause.
